Getting Started

Basic Crochet Supplies

  • Yarn
  • A crochet hook
  • Scissos
  • A yarn needle (for weaving in ends)

Choosing Yarn & Hooks

When choosing a yarn for a project, you need to consider many factors. The first is fiber type (wool, cotton, bamboo, etc), as this changes the way the crocheted project will lay on. Yarn weight also changes, depending on if the crocheted item is lacework, a blanket, a garment or other items. Also consider the yarn size in conjunction with your hook, and the care/maintanence your yarn requires.

When choosing a hook to use or purchase, there are multiple factors to consider. Hooks come in aluminum or metal, plastic, and wood, so it is important to find what feels most comfortable and smooth when using each kind personally. Hooks also come in simpler or more ergonomic forms, and it is imporant the hook size you choose is compatible with the yarn size you are using, (within +/- 1 mm of the yarn).

Basic Stitches

  • Chain (ch): The foundation of most crochet projects.
  • Single Crochet (sc): A short, tight stitch great for sturdy fabric.
  • Double Crochet (dc): A taller stitch that creates a softer fabric.
  • Turning and Starting a New Row: After completing a row, you create turning chains to reach the height of the next row before continuing.
